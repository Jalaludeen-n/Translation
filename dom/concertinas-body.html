A concertina is a free-reed musical instrument, from the same family of instruments as the accordion and the harmonica. It consists of bellows that expand and contract, with buttons (or keys) on both ends, which push air through the reeds to make a note. It was originally invented in the UK but also developed independently in Germany. As a result, today we have two popular types of concertina: the 30 key Anglo concertina and the English concertina.
