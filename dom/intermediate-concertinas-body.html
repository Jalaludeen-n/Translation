Who Should Choose an Intermediate Concertina?

If you've already spent time learning on a beginner concertina and feel ready to explore more challenging tunes, increase your speed, or refine your ornamentation, an intermediate concertina is the next logical step. It's ideal for players who have built a solid foundation and want an instrument that responds with greater sensitivity, dynamic range, and tonal clarity.

An intermediate concertina is the right choice for:

  • Players who have outgrown their beginner concertina and need a more responsive instrument

  • Musicians preparing for regular session playing or live performance

  • Students progressing through structured concertina lessons

  • Hobbyists ready to invest in better tone and playing comfort

  • Those who want professional features without the full cost of a premium concertina

Whether you're working through more advanced Irish traditional tunes or simply want to enjoy a smoother, more dynamic playing experience, an intermediate concertina will support your growth and keep up with your musical ambitions.

Frequently Asked Questions about Intermediate Concertinas

What is the difference between a beginner and intermediate concertina? +

Beginner concertinas are designed for ease of use and affordability, making them ideal for new players. Intermediate concertinas offer upgraded components—like better reeds, faster key action, and improved tone—making them perfect for players ready to develop more advanced skills.

Is an intermediate concertina suitable for Irish traditional music? +

Yes! In fact, many intermediate players choose a 30-button Anglo concertina in C/G specifically because it’s ideal for reels, jigs, and ornamentation. This layout is standard in Irish traditional music and helps players explore more complex phrasing and technique.

Do I need to upgrade from beginner to intermediate? +

If you find that your current concertina is limiting your speed, dynamics, or ability to express ornamentation clearly, then yes—moving to an intermediate concertina will make a noticeable difference in your playing.
