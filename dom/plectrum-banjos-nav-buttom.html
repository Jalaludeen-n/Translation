Plectrum banjos, known for their 22-inch scale lengths, were notably advantageous for musicians originating from the tradition of five-string banjos. The shorter tenor banjo (with four strings instead) tuned in fifths (CDGA), made chord changes more convenient on the lower fretboard. Consequently, it emerged as the preferred option for individuals approaching the instrument from diverse backgrounds, particularly for playing dance music and dixieland jazz.
