Signature F-Style Mandolin

McNeela Signature F-Style Mandolin

 

Take your Mandolin playing to the next level with our brand new McNeela F-Style Mandolin. Tailor-made for ambitious musicians and seasoned professionals, this outstanding instrument promises to bring your performance to the next level.

This New McNeela premium instrument is committed to giving both an excellent sound and performance when played. Plus, its versatility across genres like bluegrass, folk, blues, country and traditional Irish music makes this mandolin an indispensable member to any serious mandolin player’s arsenal.

KEY FEATURES OF THE MCNEELA PREMIUM F-STYLE MANDOLIN

KEY FEATURES

  • Tuned to traditional Irish GDAE.
  • Complete with 21 high-grade frets.
  • Nickel tailpiece for enhanced sustain.
  • Solid spruce top with flamed maple sides.
  • Fingerboard and bridge made of quality ebony.
  • 8 strings for a full-bodied sound.
  • Superior machine tuners for precision tuning.
  • Classic F style sound holes for resonance.
  • Provides resonant, sustained tone.

Superior Craftsmanship and Durability

At McNeela, we take great pride in the high grade materials we use in our instruments, especially in this one, which features a spruce top with flamed maple sides and back, and an ebony fretboard and bridge to boot. The result is an instrument that feels as good as it looks, designed for comfort and built to last.

Quality Counts

We believe that high quality instruments are truly a better investment for any musician. At McNeela, we guarantee that all of our instruments are made from the highest quality materials and with the necessary passion to ensure longevity and pristine sound from all of our traditional Irish instruments.

F-Hole Mandolins vs Oval Hole Mandolins

Aside from the aesthetical differences, F-hole mandolins will usually have a brighter shimmery tone with more volume and a sharper attack in comparison to their oval hole counterparts. As a result, F-hole mandolins tend to offer a more percussive sound.
 
However, the higher the craftsmanship is, the less differences in sound quality are.
