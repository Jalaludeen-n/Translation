 The Freedom 5-String Banjo

"A beautiful instrument, from construction to sound. Playing it is pure pleasure." - Davor T.

The Freedom 5-String Banjo is designed for musicians who demand exceptional sound and are ready for a premium instrument that can keep up and stand out. Its mahogany neck and resonator give a deep, warm tone that fills the room and captures attention.

Inspired by the bluegrass tradition, the 22-fret neck unlocks a wider range of musical possibilities. From the driving rhythms of bluegrass to the catchy melodies of old-time music, this banjo delivers power and playability. The Gibson-inspired flange and mahogany rim combine for a sound that is clear, bright, and perfectly resonant.

If you’re ready to step up from the limits of a beginner’s instrument or want to skip that stage entirely, this is the banjo you need!

Why Does It Sound So Good?

The mahogany neck, rim, and resonator give this banjo a naturally warm, rich tone, the reliable D’Addario strings add a brightness, and the Remo head ensures brightness and clarity where it counts. The result is a sound that projects with power and nuance, ideal for bluegrass solos, folk backing, or just filling your living room with music.

Ready for Bluegrass and Beyond

With a 22-fret neck, quality resonator, and 5 D’Addario strings tuned to Open G, this banjo opens up a wide melodic range for your favourite Bluegrass tunes. It’s also perfect for Scruggs-style picking, clawhammer tunes, and even Irish folk accompaniment. A versatile voice that adapts to the music you love.

The Real Impact of a Quality Resonator

The resonator is the power behind your banjo’s voice. It directs the sound out into the room, giving your playing the volume and clarity it deserves. The mahogany resonator gives a real depth and richness, providing a fuller tone that carries beautifully.

In Tune in No time

Geared planetary tuners mean smoother, faster, and more accurate tuning for fewer interruptions to your playing. Combined with 30 steel brackets and a nickel tailpiece, this banjo stays locked in and ready to perform, wherever and whenever you play.

Why Players are Loving the Freedom

The Freedom 5-String Banjo is as beautiful as it is powerful. Its mahogany neck and resonator give it a classic, elegant look, while the rosewood fingerboard with decorative inlays and Remo skin head ensure both style and superior sound quality.

Players tell us they keep picking it up just because it feels that good, and that’s the best habit you can build. It’s the perfect blend of form and function.

Musician Setup Makes All the Difference

Each Freedom Banjo is professionally set up by our workshop team before it leaves McNeela. That means smooth action, no buzzing, and a banjo that plays clean right out of the box. No guesswork. Just great music.

Designed for Growth, Not Outgrown

Whether you're upgrading from your first banjo or want to start off strong with an instrument that’s there for the long haul, this is a banjo that starts strong and grows with you. It’s made to serve you well from your first chords all the way through to more advanced styles and live performances.

The Freedom 5 String Banjo (Bluegrass, 22 Fret)
I’ve played banjos for over 40 years and have strummed many dozens of them. So I am glad to say the Freedom model I now own is a beautiful instrument in both its looks and sound. I live in California USA, so had an expert luthier here look it over. Not only did he concur, but admired its well-constructed durability and very reasonable price, about half or less of an American-made banjo of comparable quality. Its lack of a tone ring is a preference for me because of the decreased weight and mellower sound, but still plenty loud enough. Thus it works well with both bluegrass and folk songs. The gig bag is sturdy and well-made. I thus feel no need for a hard-she’ll case.. It’s great fun in California to own a banjo from Ireland! When it arrived, it needed virtually no setup after a trip of some six thousand miles!

I wasn't sure what to expect when I ordered this banjo online. (Not sure of the model number). My Deering banjo has a slightly wider fingerboard at the nut, but it didn't take long to get used to this narrower FB.
