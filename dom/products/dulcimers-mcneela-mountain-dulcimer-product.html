McNeela Mountain Dulcimer

“Love it. Easy to tune and play!” - Douglas K.

The McNeela Mountain Dulcimer is a lap-play instrument deeply rooted in Appalachian tradition. One of North America’s most charming instruments, it makes playing authentic folk music delightfully easy.

Built with a spruce body and four steel strings, it produces a warm, rich voice that blends seamlessly with other folk instruments or plays beautifully all on its own. Plus, the helpful diatonic fret layout keeps things simple so you can focus on the music.

Whether you’re strumming chords, picking out melodies, or experimenting with a ‘noter’ stick for that traditional Appalachian playing style and drone effect, you’ll be playing toe-tapping tunes with confidence.

Easy to learn and with a truly unique sound, it packs plenty of charm for beginners through seasoned players.

McNeela Mountain Dulcimer
More accurately, a 3.5 star! This is a lovely instrument and a great sound, a really great choice if you want to broaden your stringed instrument range and easy to write music with. Considering all that, this price is extremely good for a brand new insturment. There are a few areas on this product that do drop its rating for me. Firstly, there is no protection on the wood surface as the strings come out of the channel on the headstock, which then feed into the tuning pegs. This means the strings sink into the wood, reducing the smoothness of the tuning. I have managed to fix this issue myself, but a player with little exerpeince with stringed instruments wouldn't know how to fix themslves. On the first and second frets, I have to place my finigers on extremly specigfic points on the fret otherwise the strings just buzz and ruins the sound. and as far as a I can tell, there is no varnish on the neck which increases risk of damage from moisture, dirt, and warping. For the price, I can understand why these corners may have been cut, or just overlooked, and overall is a great addition to my instrumnts but these areas do affect it's rating and quality. Hope this all helps
