The Sparrow English Concertina

“Great quality and perfect for a beginner.” - Corban V.

The Sparrow Concertina is a 30-button English Concertina in the key of C that’s been designed specifically with beginners in mind.

The difference between an English and an Anglo concertina is that English concertinas play the same note, regardless of whether you’re pushing or pulling the bellows. Whereas an Anglo concertina plays two different notes, depending on whether you’re pushing or pulling.

We've given this instrument carefully selected steel reeds that are perfectly suited for producing the sound and tone you need for exploring traditional Irish music.

This concertina is a lightweight, compact, and beautifully crafted instrument that’s easy to get the hang off and comfortable to hold. This is a quality, entry-level concertina at an affordable price.

The Sparrow English Concertina
I adore this instrument. It's incredibly well made and the price makes it an incredibly accessible instrument. Here are some pros and cons. Pros - The bellows provide minute control over articulation. - The manner in which the keys are spread over both hands makes it easy to find melodies that are deeply affecting. - The close spacing of the keys allows for multiple keys to be pushed simultaneously with a single finger. - The layout of the keys is actually quite natural once you get the hang of it. Cons - It's rather easy to lose a key inside the concertina, or stick a key so that it doesn't return to it's default position. In order to fix this it requires at least 6 screws to be unscrewed, the key to be placed back on it's lever, and then those 6 screws of course need to be screwed back in. This can be a bit time consuming - though I understand it isn't unusual as far as concertinas go! I imagine that it's rather difficult to solve this particular issue with the design used, as if you make the keys stick to their levers tighter they might get stuck forever. I'm no trained musician and I haven't yet seen any tutorials (though that's on me!) and I can vouch for the beauty of this instrument - in the short time I've had it, I've been able to make some beautiful melodies that have helped me through a tough time. I'm looking forward to improving my skill with this instrument, and perusing the instructional material that McNeela has made available. The concertina is a brilliant instrument, it can be used to produce so much varied output - it feels like an entire orchestra in my (relatively unskilled) hands! I'd go so far as to say that there's something deeply familiar about it, though I can't place when I'd have used one in the past. I'd like to try a bisonoric concertina in the future! Also, I imagine that the updated version of this instrument is indeed better in many ways, and so if you're looking for a first concertina it might be better to get one of those!
