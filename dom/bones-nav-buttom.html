The percussive bones, also known as rhythm bones, stand as Ireland's oldest percussive Irish folk instrument. Nowadays, this versatile Irish percussion instrument boasts a modern design that optimizes comfort, playability, and a diverse range of rhythms and sounds. Traditionally, certain sections of large rib bones and lower leg bones served as the primary materials for these instruments, although wooden sticks shaped like true bones and metal bones are now more common.
