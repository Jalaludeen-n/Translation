Frequently Asked Questions about Irish Banjos

What is the difference between an Irish tenor banjo and a regular banjo? +

The Irish tenor banjo features 4 strings, tuned GDAE – the same as an Irish fiddle. This specific string of a banjo setup makes it perfect for playing fast, melodic traditional Irish tunes, unlike the 5-string banjo used in bluegrass.

What is the best Irish banjo for beginners? +

The best Irish banjo for beginners is the Celt Banjo – lightweight, easy to play, and crafted for clear tone. It’s the most popular choice among learners and offers excellent value for money without sacrificing quality or performance.

How to Choose the Right Irish Tenor Banjo +

If you're just starting out, a 17-fret Irish tenor banjo is ideal – it's lighter, more compact, and easier to handle for beginners. If you're an intermediate or advanced player looking for greater volume and resonance, a 19-fret banjo offers extended range and projection, perfect for session playing.
